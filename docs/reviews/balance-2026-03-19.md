### Observations

- **Paladin is the highest-power hero at every level** despite being the tank. This is driven by massive HP (HP/5 contribution) and huge DEF. At L15 she is 128.5 power vs Warrior 121.4.
- **Ranger and Mage start at identical 29.0 power** but diverge: Mage pulls ahead by L10 due to +3 ATK growth. Their roles are distinct (SPD vs ATK) so the parity at L1 is fine, but Ranger's late-game power (106.0) is noticeably the weakest.
- **Damage output divergence is extreme.** At L15, Mage deals `57 - DEF/2` while Paladin deals `22 - DEF/2`. Paladin's ATK growth of 1/level makes her nearly useless offensively. Against Elder Dragon (DEF 20), Paladin deals `max(1, 22 - 10) = 12` while Mage deals `57 - 10 = 47`. Paladin is essentially a pure tank, which is fine IF the targeting AI supports it (currently targets highest HP enemy, not the player).
- **Speed tiers are well-separated:** Ranger (54) >> Mage (36) >> Warrior (18) >> Paladin (17). Good class feel.

### Issue: Ranger Feels Weak Late

Ranger's power curve flattens relative to others. With only +2 ATK growth (same as Warrior) but much lower HP and DEF, she's a glass cannon that doesn't cannon hard enough. At L15, Ranger ATK 38 vs Warrior ATK 40 -- the "damage dealer" Ranger does LESS damage than the tank Warrior.

**Recommendation:** Increase Ranger ATK growth from 2 to 3 in `data/heroes.js`. This gives L15 Ranger ATK = 52, putting her between Warrior (40) and Mage (57), which properly reflects a damage-dealer with range flavor.

### Tier Scaling

| Tier | Avg Total Stats | Avg Craft Time | Stat/Second |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| T1   | 6.0             | 28s            | 0.214       |
| T2   | 12.0            | 68s            | 0.176       |

Stats-per-second DECREASES with tier, which is correct for an idle game (higher tiers = more AFK time for better absolute rewards). The T1-to-T2 jump is exactly 2x in stats; T2-to-T3 is 2.2x. This is a smooth progression.

### Rarity & Level Multipliers

At max rarity (Epic 2.5x) and max level (Lv3 1.8x), total multiplier = 4.5x.

A Lv3 Epic Mithril Blade: ATK = 22 * 4.5 = 99, DEF = 4 * 4.5 = 18, SPD = 3 * 4.5 = 13.5. Total = 130.5 stats. This is enormously powerful. A single maxed T3 weapon outclasses everything else combined.

A Lv3 Epic T1 Wooden Sword: ATK = 22.5, DEF = 0, SPD = 4.5 = 27 stats. This is roughly equal to a base T3 item (26.7 avg). This means a perfectly rolled T1 rivals a common T3. This could be a problem depending on how long it takes to get an Epic, but since T1 drops come from early content, it's unlikely players get Epic T1 drops that matter by the time they have T3.

### Issues

1. **Mithril Blade is best-in-slot by too wide a margin.** It has the most stats, fastest craft time, and lowest material cost among T3. There is no reason to ever craft Mystic Amulet, Dragonscale Armor, or Guardian Plate unless you specifically need DEF.

2. **Mystic Amulet is a trap.** 300 gold + 8 gems for 24 total stats vs Mithril Blade's 150 gold + 3 gems for 29 stats. The "balanced stats" flavor doesn't justify 2x the cost for fewer stats.

3. **Healer's Charm is strictly worse** than Arcane Tome (9 vs 14 stats, same gem cost tier). No reason to craft it.

4. **Herb Pouch is underpowered** at T1 -- only 4 stats vs 6-7 for other T1 items.

5. **Warhammer has no gem cost** which makes it a bizarre T3 outlier. Either it's intentionally "farmable" or it's a data error.

### Recommendations

In `data/recipes.js`:

- **Mystic Amulet:** Reduce cost to gold 200, gems 5. Increase stats to ATK:10 DEF:10 SPD:10 (total 30) to justify "jack of all trades" premium.
- **Healer's Charm:** Increase DEF to 8 and SPD to 5 (total 13) to differentiate from Arcane Tome as a support item.
- **Herb Pouch:** Increase SPD to 4 (total 5) or add a small DEF bump to 2 (total 6).
- **Mithril Blade:** Increase craft time to 210s and gem cost to 5 to bring in line.
- **Warhammer:** Add gems 2-3 cost to prevent it being a free-farm T3.

### Critical Problem: Bosses Are Pushovers

The core issue is that **bosses are single enemies facing multiple heroes**. The action economy means heroes get 2-4 attacks per boss attack. Bosses need either:
1. Much higher stats (especially HP)
2. Multi-target attacks
3. Accompanying minions

Additionally, the power requirement for expeditions is trivially exceeded. The Dragon Boss requires P130 but a mid-level 4-hero party easily has P271+. Power requirements don't gate content meaningfully.

### Recommendations

In `data/enemies.js`:
- **Double boss HP across the board.** Treant Elder 80->160, Stone Golem 100->200, Sandworm Queen 120->240, Frost Dragon 140->300, Elder Dragon 200->500.
- **Increase boss ATK by ~30%.** Elder Dragon 28->36, Frost Dragon 22->28, etc.

In `data/expeditions.js`:
- **Increase power requirements for later expeditions.** Dragon Boss P130->P200, Frostpeak Boss P90->P140, Dusthaven Boss P75->P110.

In `lib/combat.js`:
- Consider adding a boss mechanic where bosses attack ALL heroes each turn (AoE), or attack twice per round. This would make boss fights feel like actual boss fights.

## 5. Economy Flow Analysis

### Resource Generation (per minute, passive)

| Resource | Rate/min | Rate/hr | Notes          |
|----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| Wood     | 2.0      | 120     |                |
| Stone    | 1.0      | 60      |                |
| Iron     | 0.5      | 30      |                |
| Herbs    | 0.3      | 18      |                |
| Gold     | 1.0      | 60      |                |
| Gems     | 0.0      | 0       | Unlock at L15  |

### Early Game (L1-L5): The Herb Bottleneck

T1 recipes need: Wood (abundant at 2/min), Stone (moderate), Iron (scarce), Herbs (very scarce at 0.3/min).

**Leather Vest** needs 5 herbs = 16.7 minutes of passive herb generation.
**Herb Pouch** needs 15 herbs = 50 minutes of passive herb generation.
**Apprentice Staff** needs 10 herbs = 33.3 minutes of passive herb generation.

Meanwhile, wood accumulates 4x faster than herbs. A player will have 100 wood before they have 15 herbs. Wood has no sink besides T1 recipes (20 wood = 10 min accumulation).

**Gold income is 1.0/min** but the first gold sink (Iron Sword: 30 gold) doesn't appear until T2. Gold piles up with nothing to spend it on early.

Hero leveling costs: L1->L2 = 50g, L2->L3 = 100g, ..., L5 total = 750g. At 60g/hr passive, that's 12.5 hours for L5 from gold alone. This seems steep but expedition gold helps significantly.

### Mid Game (L5-L10): The Iron/Gem Squeeze

T2 recipes need iron (20-25 per recipe) and gems (1-2 per recipe).

Iron at 0.5/min = 30/hr. One Iron Sword (20 iron) = 40 min of passive iron generation.
Gems at 0/min passive = 100% expedition-dependent. Only Dark Forest (1-2 gems, 10 min, L7), Iron Mines (2-4, 15 min, L8), and bosses drop gems.

Expected gems/hr from Dark Forest: 1.5 gems / 10min * 1.5x victory = 13.5 gems/hr. This is decent.

Gold sinks ramp up: T2 recipes cost 30-80 gold each. Hero leveling L5->L10 = sum(250,300,350,400,450) = 1750g per hero. With 2-3 heroes that's 3500-5250g.

### Late Game (L10-L15): The Gem Wall

T3 recipes need 3-8 gems each. With gems only from expeditions, a player needs:

Mithril Blade: 3 gems. From Glacier Cave (5-9 gems per 15min run) = roughly 1 run.
Mystic Amulet: 8 gems = ~2 Glacier Cave runs = 30 min.
Dragonscale Armor: 5 gems = ~1 Glacier Cave run.

This seems reasonable, but the player needs to be L11+ to access Glacier Cave. Before that, gem income is limited to 1-4 gems per run from lower-tier expeditions.

Hero leveling L10->L15 = sum(500,550,600,650,700) = 3000g per hero. With 3-4 heroes = 9000-12000g total. At ~60g/hr passive + expedition income (~200g/hr from mid-tier), this is roughly 30-40 hours. That's a lot for a casual game.

### Economy Issues

1. **Herbs are the early-game bottleneck.** At 0.3/min, herbs gate crafting progression for 3 of 5 T1 recipes. Players will feel stuck waiting for herbs while wood piles up unused.

2. **Gold has no early sink.** Between L1-L4, the only gold sink is hero leveling (50-200g each). Gold accumulates with nothing to spend on.

3. **Gem generation rate of 0/min even after L15 unlock** seems like a data note says "unlock L15" but rate is 0. If gems truly generate at 0/min passively even after L15, players are 100% expedition-dependent for gems forever. This is probably intentional but worth verifying.

4. **Hero leveling is extremely expensive late game.** L14->L15 costs 750g PER HERO. With 4 heroes that's 3000g for a single level, at 60g/hr passive = 50 hours. This is the primary gold sink and it might be too steep.

### Recommendations

In `data/resources.js`:
- **Increase herb generation to 0.5/min.** This smooths early-game pacing without breaking anything.
- **Add passive gem generation after L15: 0.05/min** (3/hr). This gives a trickle that supplements expeditions.

In `data/heroes.js` or `data/progression.js`:
- **Change hero level cost formula** from `level * 50` to `level * 40` to reduce late-game gold pressure, OR add a diminishing cost curve like `40 + level * 30`.

The Paladin unlock at L15 is essentially post-game content since L15 is also the prestige threshold. A player unlocks Paladin only to immediately prestige? This feels bad.

### Recommendations

In `data/progression.js`:
- **Reduce L12-L15 XP requirements by ~25%.** L13: 9000->7500, L14: 11500->9500, L15: 14500->12000. This brings total playtime closer to 90 hours.

In `data/heroes.js`:
- **Move Paladin unlock to L12** (from L15). This gives players time to enjoy the full party before prestige becomes available.

## 10. Appendix: Power Formula Sanity Check

Current formula: `power = atk + def + spd/2 + hp/5`

This weights ATK and DEF equally, SPD at half, HP at one-fifth. In actual combat:
- ATK directly maps to damage output (minus enemy DEF/2)
- DEF halves incoming damage (but only matters if you're being hit)
- SPD determines turn order (binary advantage, not linear)
- HP is a health pool

The problem: **DEF is overvalued in the power formula relative to its combat impact.** In combat, DEF reduces incoming damage by DEF/2. The Paladin's 56 DEF at L15 means enemies deal `ATK - 28` damage. Against Elder Dragon (ATK 28), that's 0 damage (floored to 1). DEF effectively has hard diminishing returns once it exceeds `2 * enemy ATK`, but the power formula counts it linearly.

This means the power gating system (expedition requirements) treats Paladin as the strongest hero (128.5 power) when she actually contributes the least in combat (1 damage/turn against high-DEF bosses).

**Recommendation:** Consider revising power formula to `atk*1.2 + def*0.8 + spd/2 + hp/5` to slightly favor offensive contribution, or keep as-is since it serves as a gating metric rather than a combat effectiveness metric.
